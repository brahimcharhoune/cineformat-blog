10 Mistakes Beginner Screenwriters Always Make (And How to Fix Them)

If you're starting your screenwriting journey, you're already ahead of most people because you actually started. But here’s the truth: most beginner screenwriters make the same mistakes… and those mistakes can instantly make your script look amateur, even if your idea is great. Let’s break down the 10 most common ones and how to fix them.

1. Ignoring Proper Screenplay Formatting

This is the fastest way to get your script rejected.

Producers and readers expect industry-standard formatting. If your margins, dialogue, or scene headings are wrong, your script may not even be read.

Fix:
Learn basic formatting rules or use tools that automatically format while you write.

2. Writing Without a Clear Structure

Many beginners jump straight into writing without planning.

The result? A messy story with no direction.

Fix:
Follow a simple 3-act structure:

  • Beginning (setup)
  • Middle (conflict)
  • End (resolution)

3. Overwriting Descriptions

Beginners often write long paragraphs describing every detail.

Fix:

Keep descriptions short, visual, and to the point.
If it can’t be seen on screen, don’t write it.

4. Weak or Unrealistic Dialogue

Dialogue that feels fake or forced is very common.

Characters shouldn’t sound like they’re explaining the plot.

Fix:

  • Keep dialogue natural
  • Read it out loud
  • Cut unnecessary lines

5. Too Many Characters

Adding too many characters makes your script confusing.

Readers won’t remember who’s who.

Fix:

Focus on a small number of important characters and develop them well.

6. Lack of Conflict

No conflict = no story.

If everything goes smoothly, your script becomes boring.

Fix:

 Every scene should have tension, obstacles, or stakes.

7. Starting Too Late (or Too Early)

Some scripts start with unnecessary scenes.

Others jump too fast into the story without setup.

Fix:
Start as close as possible to the main conflict.

8. Not Understanding Scene Purpose

 Many beginners write scenes that don’t move the story forward.
Fix:
Ask yourself:
  • Does this scene advance the plot?
  • Does it reveal something important?
If not, cut it.

9. Formatting Manually (and Getting It Wrong)

Trying to format everything manually in tools like Google Docs often leads to errors:
  • Dialogue misaligned
  • Scene headings inconsistent
  • Spacing issues
Fix:
Learn basic formatting rules or follow a step-by-step guide on how to format a screenplay properly in Google Docs.

10. Never Finishing the Script

This is the biggest mistake.

Many beginners start scripts… but never finish them.

Fix:

  • Don’t aim for perfection
  • Finish the first draft no matter what
  • You can always improve later

Final Thoughts

Every screenwriter makes mistakes—it’s part of the process.

What separates beginners from professionals is simple:

👉 Professionals fix their mistakes and keep going.

If you focus on improving just these 10 areas, your script will already be better than most beginners.

And most importantly…

Finish your script.
