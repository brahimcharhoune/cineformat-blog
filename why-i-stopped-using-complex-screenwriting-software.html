Why I Stopped Using Complex Screenwriting Software

For a long time, I believed that to write a “real” screenplay, I needed professional, complex screenwriting software. You’ve probably heard the same advice: “Use industry-standard tools or your script won’t be taken seriously.” So I tried them. I explored tools like Final Draft and Celtx, expecting them to make my writing process faster and better. But something unexpected happened… They actually slowed me down.

The Problem With Complex Tools

At first, everything looks powerful:

  • Dozens of features
  • Advanced formatting options
  • Professional templates

But for beginners (and even many experienced writers), this becomes a problem.

Instead of focusing on your story, you spend time:

  • Learning the interface
  • Fixing settings
  • Navigating menus
Writing starts to feel like technical work instead of creative work.

I Was Spending More Time Formatting Than Writing

Ironically, the thing these tools promise to solve—formatting—became a distraction.

I found myself constantly:

  • Breaking my writing flow to fix formatting
  • Losing ideas while adjusting technical details
  • Clicking between different elements instead of focusing on the story
Instead of writing scenes, I was managing the tool. And that killed my momentum.

Creativity Needs Simplicity

Great ideas don’t come from complicated systems.

They come when you’re:

  • Writing freely
  • Thinking about characters
  • Exploring dialogue
The more friction you have, the harder it is to stay in that creative flow.

Why I Switched to Simpler Tools

At some point, I decided to try something different.

I went back to basics using Google Docs.

At first, it felt “less professional.”

But very quickly, I noticed something important:

👉 I was writing more.

No complex setup. No distractions. Just a blank page and ideas.

But There Was One Big Problem…

Formatting.

Writing in Google Docs is easy…
But formatting a screenplay manually?

  • Dialogue gets misaligned
  • Scene headings become inconsistent
  • Spacing breaks when exporting

I was back to wasting time again.

The Simple Solution

Instead of going back to complex software, I looked for a middle ground:

👉 Keep writing simple
👉 Automate formatting

That’s when everything changed.

Using tools that handle formatting automatically inside Google Docs allowed me to:

  • Focus on writing
  • Keep industry-standard format
  • Save hours of manual work

What Actually Matters in Screenwriting

After trying both extremes, I realized something:

Producers don’t care what tool you use.

They care about:

  • Your story
  • Your characters
  • Your structure

Formatting matters but only to the extent that it looks professional.

Nothing more.

Final Thoughts

Complex screenwriting software isn’t bad.

But it’s not always necessary especially if it slows you down.

Sometimes, the best thing you can do is simplify your process.

Because at the end of the day…

👉 The best script is the one you actually finish.
